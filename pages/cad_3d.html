I've been making 3D models with CAD software since I was in middle school, and over the years I've grown to really enjoy it. In 2024, I bought a 3D printer, and I've been using it almost nonstop since! I've been modeling little doodads for various purposes and printing them. Below are a few of the things I've made!

Baldwin Organ Keys

When two of the keys on my beloved 1962 Baldwin Orgasonic electric organ were broken by a falling radio, I took matters into my own hands! With the pieces of the broken keys, I used Autodesk Fusion and a caliper to create very accurate models that I was able to print and install onto my organ. The models are parameterized, so that by only changing a few numbers I can print any of the keys! I have already uploaded the finished keys to Thingiverse, but I'll host them here as well for your convenience! SD Card Adapter Holder

My lovely 3D Printer (dubbed Gerald) suffers from one major inconvenience: it takes a micro SD card. My PC (and pretty much any computer I'd be trying to print from) only has a full-sized SD card reader. I got sick of always needing to find the adapter, so I decided to do the closest thing to giving Gerald a full sized SD card reader I could with my immediate surroundings: I created a little holder so Gerald would always have an adapter on him. This has made things much simpler, as Gerald is always conveniently holding the micro SD to full-sized SD adapter! I modelled this card holder such that it would slot right into the frame of the printer. Gerald is an AnyCubic Kobra 2, so in case you've got the same printer, a download link to the STL is provided below!
